Biography

The Full Story

I'm a first-year Electrical Engineering student at the University of Waterloo, one of Canada's top engineering programs. My journey into hardware started with curiosity — I wanted to understand how the devices around me actually worked, down to the electron level.

That curiosity led me to join the Orbital CubeSat Team, where I work with an incredible group to build and test a satellite from scratch. Designing power systems, validating performance in simulation, then seeing those numbers match real hardware — that's the part that gets me excited.

My co-op at Electrans Technology turned that curiosity into professional experience. I went from writing LTSpice sims to having my electrical validation work on a product that's now retailed on DigiKey. I also cut a critical thermal shutdown problem down to near-zero by rethinking the power architecture — a 90.2% reduction in heat dissipation. Those are the wins that stick.

Outside of engineering, I'm someone who values getting things done right. I read broadly, play soccer competitively, and recently discovered that cooking is surprisingly similar to engineering — both reward patience and precision.
