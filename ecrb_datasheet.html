CAN / J1939 Hardware Validation Technical Writing Commercial Launch
±58V fault protection verified Listed on DigiKey J1939 CAN protocol

The ETL CAN Hitch is a J1939 CAN Bridge designed by Electrans Technology for agricultural and industrial vehicle applications. My role was to perform the electrical validation required to finalize the product for commercial release, and to author the technical datasheet that would serve as the primary customer integration document.

The critical validation task was verifying the ±58V fault protection circuitry. In agricultural environments, CAN bus lines are routinely exposed to load dump transients and wiring faults that can exceed the absolute maximum ratings of unprotected transceivers. Verifying that the protection circuitry clamped these events correctly — without corrupting bus traffic — was essential for customer confidence and safety compliance.

Alongside the electrical validation, I authored the complete technical datasheet from scratch. This included specifying electrical characteristics tables, pin descriptions, application circuits, recommended PCB layout guidelines, and fault conditions. The goal was to give integrators everything they needed to design the CAN Hitch into their system without requiring direct support from Electrans.

The product launched on DigiKey, making it accessible to engineers worldwide. This project was a direct lesson in what it takes to take hardware from a functional prototype to a commercially released, customer-integrated product.

Key Technologies

  • CAN / J1939 Protocol: Validated bus traffic, arbitration, and bridge behaviour across CAN segments
  • Fault Protection Testing: Verified ±58V transient clamping, reverse polarity protection, and bus fault conditions
  • Oscilloscope Analysis: Captured CAN signal integrity, rise/fall times, and eye diagram characterization
  • Technical Documentation: Authored complete datasheet — specs tables, timing diagrams, application circuits, footprint drawings
