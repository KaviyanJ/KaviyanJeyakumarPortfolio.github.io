Buck Converter
Simulation

I developed a high-fidelity LTspice simulation to model and analyze a buck converter's power stage and feedback loop. The primary goal was to optimize its real-world performance by validating power efficiency, transient response, and stability before committing to the final PCB design.

RescueTouch Emergency
System

I designed and developed a two-board emergency system using STM32 microcontrollers, handling both the schematic design in KiCad and the C++ firmware development. The project's main goal was to create a robust assistive device by establishing reliable UART communication between a Braille-labeled input board and an LCD output board.

Solar Cell Board
Assembly

I was responsible for the hands-on assembly of the solar cell power PCB, which involved populating the board with all the specified components from the design. The objective was to bridge the gap between simulation and reality, validating that the physical circuit's performance metrics aligned with the results we had predicted in our LTspice models.
